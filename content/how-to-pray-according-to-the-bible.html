How to Pray According to the Bible (Simple Guide)

The Bible teaches prayer as honest, direct conversation with God — not a performance requiring special words. Jesus gave the Lord's Prayer in Matthew 6:9-13 as a framework: begin with praise, bring real needs, confess sin, surrender your will, and close with thanksgiving. Anyone can pray biblically starting today, regardless of experience or background.

What does the Bible say about how to pray?

  1. Matthew 6:9-13 — Jesus teaches the Lord's Prayer as a model: address God as Father, honor his name, pray for his kingdom, ask for daily provision, seek forgiveness, and ask for protection from evil. Use it as a template, not just a recitation.
  2. Philippians 4:6 — "In everything, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God." Thanksgiving is embedded in the ask — not added as a formality but woven into how you bring requests.
  3. 1 John 5:14 — "If we ask anything according to his will, he hears us." Praying according to God's will is not a limiting condition — it is an invitation to align your desires with what is actually good and lasting.
  4. James 5:16 — "The prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective." Prayer is not passive — it is described as active and effective when brought by someone walking honestly before God.
  5. Luke 18:1 — Jesus told a parable about praying "always and not giving up." Persistent prayer is taught explicitly, not as a sign God is reluctant but as a sign of genuine dependence and trust.

How to pray according to the Bible in five practical steps

  1. Start with praise — name who God is before naming what you need: faithful, merciful, present, all-knowing.
  2. Confess honestly — name where you missed the mark this day without vagueness or self-excuse; receive forgiveness consciously.
  3. Ask specifically — bring real, concrete requests rather than general hopes; Philippians 4:6 says "everything" is appropriate to bring.
  4. Surrender the outcome — close specific requests with "not my will, but yours" (Luke 22:42); this is trust, not passivity.
  5. End with thanksgiving — even brief, specific thanks before finishing reorients the heart from anxiety toward trust.

Does prayer require special words or religious training?

No. Jesus warned against praying with impressive-sounding words for human audiences (Matthew 6:5-7). The tax collector in Luke 18:13 prayed only "God, have mercy on me, a sinner" and was heard. Biblical prayer values honesty and humility over eloquence. If you can talk, you can pray — start with what is true about your situation and who you believe God to be.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Bible say about how to pray?

Matthew 6:9-13 gives the Lord's Prayer as a framework: begin with God's name and kingdom, bring daily needs, seek forgiveness while forgiving others, and ask for protection from evil. Philippians 4:6 adds thanksgiving to every request. 1 John 5:14 grounds prayer in God's will. Together they give a complete practical model for biblical prayer.

Does God hear all prayers or only certain kinds?

1 John 5:14 says God hears us when we ask according to his will. James 4:3 notes that self-centered motives can block answered prayer. But Psalm 34:17-18 shows God near to those who are brokenhearted and crying out. God hears honest prayer from real people in real need — even when the theology is imperfect and the words are stumbling.

What is the correct posture for prayer in the Bible?

The Bible shows people praying standing (1 Kings 8:22), kneeling (Ephesians 3:14), prostrate (Matthew 26:39), sitting (2 Samuel 7:18), and with eyes open or closed. No single posture is required. The more consistent biblical emphasis is on the posture of the heart — humility, honesty, and expectant faith — rather than any physical position.

How long should a prayer be according to the Bible?

Jesus warned against long prayers offered to impress others (Matthew 6:7). The Lord's Prayer itself is about sixty words. But Jesus also spent entire nights in prayer (Luke 6:12). Length is not the measure — honesty and faith are. A brief, direct prayer from a sincere heart is received the same as a long eloquent one. Quality over performance.

Can I pray anywhere, or does location matter?

Jesus prayed in deserts, mountains, gardens, and synagogues. He commended private prayer in a room (Matthew 6:6) and promised presence where two or three gather (Matthew 18:20). Location does not determine whether God hears you. The portability of prayer is one of its great gifts — it works at a kitchen table, in a hospital, or on a commute.
