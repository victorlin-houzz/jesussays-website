Jesus Says vs Hallow — Which Christian Prayer App Is Right for You?

If you're looking for a Hallow alternative, Jesus Says is a lighter, text-based option for personalized Scripture reflection — Hallow is audio-first and built around Catholic prayer traditions, while Jesus Says responds to your specific question or moment with Bible verses and a prayer prompt.

Where Hallow excels

Hallow is purpose-built for Catholic prayer practice. Its library includes guided Rosary sessions, the Examen prayer, daily saint stories, sleep meditations, and themed prayer series. The experience is immersive and led — you press play and follow along. For someone who wants their faith practice to feel like a directed retreat, Hallow is unusually well-designed. It also supports parish and small group use through Hallow for Organizations.

Where Jesus Says is different

Jesus Says is not a curated library. It is a conversation-style reflection tool: you bring a specific situation, question, or emotional state, and the app responds with relevant Scripture and a prayer prompt. It is text-based, lightweight, and fast — suited for a moment of need rather than a planned devotional session. It does not include Catholic prayer forms, audio production, or structured content sequences.

Which one should you choose?

If your faith practice centers on Catholic prayer forms and you want guided audio to lead you through the Rosary, Examen, or saint reflections, Hallow is the right fit. If you want to bring a personal question and receive a personalized, text-based Scripture reflection, Jesus Says is built for that moment. The two apps serve genuinely different needs and can be used alongside each other.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Jesus Says a good Hallow alternative?

Yes, if you're looking for a text-based, AI-assisted Scripture reflection companion rather than audio-guided Catholic prayer. Jesus Says responds to your specific question or moment; Hallow is built around a structured library of guided prayers, Rosary meditations, and saint stories for Catholic practitioners.

What is Hallow best known for?

Hallow is known for its audio-first approach to Catholic prayer — guided Rosary, Examen, sleep meditations, and saint stories. It serves individuals, parishes, and small groups. It is a curated content library, not an AI conversation tool.

Can I use Jesus Says if I'm Catholic?

Yes. Jesus Says is a broadly Christian app grounded in Scripture. It does not include Catholic-specific prayer forms like the Rosary or Examen, but anyone who wants personalized, Scripture-based reflection can use it. For specifically Catholic prayer traditions, Hallow is more purpose-built.

Is Jesus Says free compared to Hallow?

Jesus Says is free to download and use on iPhone as of 2026-05-09. Hallow offers a limited free tier, with full access requiring a subscription. Check current pricing in each app's App Store listing, as pricing can change.

Do either of these apps replace church or pastoral care?

No. Both are designed as personal prayer companions, not replacements for church community, pastors, or professional care. Jesus Says is a companion, not a substitute for pastoral relationships, counseling, or emergency support.

Care note: Jesus Says is a prayer and Scripture reflection companion, not medical, mental health, legal, emergency, or pastoral care. If you may harm yourself or someone else, contact local emergency services or a crisis hotline immediately.
